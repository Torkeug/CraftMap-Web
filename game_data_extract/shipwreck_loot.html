Patch & Blueprint Odds by Item

Per-item odds of pulling a specific Patch or Blueprint from a shipwreck's rare loot crate — sectors grouped together wherever the odds land on the same number. Derived from data.cdb and decompiled src/logic/Loot.hx, cross-checked against a real drop.

Patch — finished component Blueprint — unlocks the recipe Bar = odds relative to the best item in its list
How the % is computed. A crate targeting (capped) level L pools every Patch/Blueprint candidate with lootLevel ∈ {L−1, L} and draws one uniformly — confirmed via raw opcodes at src/logic/Loot.hx:461-478, not an exact-level match. So P(item, lootLevel Lx) sums over crate target L ∈ {Lx, Lx+1} of: P(crate lands on L in this sector) × P(a Patch-or-Blueprint drops at all, clamp((L−2)/5,0,1)) × 50% (category split) × 1/(size of the {L−1,L} pool). Percentages shown at 2 significant figures, so identical-looking sectors may differ a hair on higher precision.
Sources. Wreck-tier mix / loot ceiling: sector.generation.wreckResGen / sector.props.maxLootLevel. Patch pool: item.type=Patch rows with their own lootLevel. Blueprint pool: craft.lootLevel rows, named "Blueprint: <output item>" per the game's own convention. Data snapshot: res.pak, 2026-07-11.
Known simplification. The Patch/Blueprint 50/50 split when both categories have an eligible candidate is an approximation of a small per-candidate weighting formula (src/logic/Loot.hx:295-317) not fully traced. Only lootLevel 2 and 10 items are truly unreachable from any wreck crate — crate targets never go below 4 or above 9, so no {L−1,L} window ever reaches level 2 or 10.
