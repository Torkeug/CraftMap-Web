Patch & Blueprint Odds by Item

Per-item odds of pulling a specific Patch or Blueprint from a shipwreck's rare loot crate — sectors grouped together wherever the odds land on the same number. Derived from data.cdb and decompiled src/logic/Loot.hx, cross-checked against a real drop.

Patch — finished component Blueprint — unlocks the recipe Bar = odds relative to the best item in its list
How the % is computed. A crate targeting (capped) level L pools every Patch/Blueprint candidate with lootLevel ∈ {L−1, L} and draws one uniformly — confirmed via raw opcodes at src/logic/Loot.hx:461-478, not an exact-level match. So P(item, lootLevel Lx) sums over crate target L ∈ {Lx, Lx+1} of: P(crate lands on L in this sector) × P(a Patch-or-Blueprint drops at all, clamp((L−2)/5,0,1)) × the real weighted category-split share (not a flat 50%, see below) × 1/(size of the {L−1,L} pool). Percentages shown at 2 significant figures, so identical-looking sectors may differ a hair on higher precision.
Sources. Wreck-tier mix / loot ceiling: sector.generation.wreckResGen / sector.props.maxLootLevel. Patch pool: item.type=Patch rows with their own lootLevel. Blueprint pool: craft.lootLevel rows that ALSO have craft.unlockType==2 (Random_Blueprint) — a recipe with lootLevel set but a different unlockType (e.g. Unique_Blueprint, a fixed quest/vendor/location source) never actually drops from a crate, confirmed via the dedicated Blueprint-candidate closure in src/logic/Loot.hx (see game_logic_notes.md Finding 15) — named "Blueprint: <output item>" per the game's own convention. Data snapshot: res.pak, 2026-07-21.
Category split. The Patch/Blueprint category split uses the real per-candidate weighting formula (src/logic/Loot.hx:295-317, fully traced — see game_logic_notes.md Finding 15): weight = max(0, 10 − |L − itl| − 2*(L − candidate's own lootLevel)), itl=5 for Patch / itl=7 for Blueprint. This is the COMPLETE model for these crates: the loot sheet rows they reference have primaryItemTypes==12 (Patch|Blueprint bits only), so the Tool/Module/ShipDecorative categories — real branches in the game's own code — never compete for this crate type's primary-item slot at all. Only lootLevel 2 and 10 items are truly unreachable from any wreck crate — crate targets never go below 4 or above 9, so no {L−1,L} window ever reaches level 2 or 10.
